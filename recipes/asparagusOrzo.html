Asparagus Orzo

  1. Home
  2. Achari Chicken
  3. Crispy Gnocchi
  1. Salt and black pepper
  2. 1 cup of orzo
  3. 1 pound of aparagus, cut thinly and sliced on a diagonal
  4. 5 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  5. 1 teaspoon lemon zest and 3 tablespoons lemon juice (from 1 large lemon)
  6. 1/2 cup panko or homemade breadcrumbs
  7. 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
  8. 1/4 cup finely grated parmesan
  9. 1/2 cup fresh dill, mint or parsley leaves (or a combination)

Step 1: Bring a medium pot of salted water to a boil. Add the orzo and cook until al dente (see orzo packaging, timing may vary). Two minutes before the orzo is done add the asparagus. Drain the orzo and asparagus.

Step 2: While the orzo and asparagus cook, make the dressing: In a large bowl, stir together 3 tablespoons olive oil and the lemon zest and juice; season to taste with salt and pepper. Add the drained orzo and asparagus and toss to coat. Set aside.

Step 3: In a reserved pot, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium (I use 4 on my stovetop). Add the panko and cook, stirring, until golden brown, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove from heat, then stir in the garlic and season with salt and pepper.

Step 4: Stir the parmesan and herbs into the orzo, taste, then season with salt, pepper, and additional lemon juice, as needed. Top with the toasted bread crumbs and more parmesan if you like. Serve warm or at room temperature.
